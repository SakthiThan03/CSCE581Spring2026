Quiz 1 Question 2 Findings:

The correlation between input pH (PH-E) and output pH (PH-S) was -0.0321. This value is very close to 0, which indicates that there no significant linear correlation between input pH and treated output pH. This indicates that output pH is not directly dependent on input pH. This PH-E is not alone a strong predictor of PH-S. A new classification column SAFE-PH-S was created, where the dataset revealed to be highly imbalanced, with only ~3% of samples classified as safe. 2 classifiers were trained: a decision tree and Logistic Regression model. Both models achieved high overall accuracy (Decision Tree: 94.34%, Logistic Regression: 97.17%). However, despite the high accuracy, both models failed to correctly identify any of the safe samples. The confusion matrices showed that all instances were predicted as “no” (unsafe), resulting in zero precision and recall for the “yes” (safe) class. This demonstrates that accuracy alone is not a reliable metric when dealing with highly imbalanced datasets. Although the models appear to perform well numerically, they are not effective for identifying safe water conditions.
