Quiz 1 Question 1 Findings:

Both Logistic Regression and Random Forest classifiers achieved moderate predictive performance. However, substantial racial disparities were observed. In the Logistic Regression model, African American defendants were predicted high risk at significantly higher rates (.523 vs .268) and experienced higher false positive rates (.314 to .182) compared to Caucasian defendants. The Random Forest model showed similar disparities. These findings indicate violations of demographic parity and equalized odds criteria. The results align with prior concerns raised about COMPAS, demonstrating that predictive models trained on criminal justice data can produce disparate error rates across racial groups. This highlights the inherent trade offs between accuracy and fairness in risk assessment systems.
