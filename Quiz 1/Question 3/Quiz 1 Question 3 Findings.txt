Quiz 1 Question 3 Findings:

The WaterAtlas dataset was filtered to include only pH measurements, and a binary column SAFE-PH was created where values were labeled “yes” if 6.5 < pH ≤ 8.5 and “no” otherwise. Using latitude and longitude as predictors, the data was split into 80% training and 20% testing with 10-fold cross-validation. The Decision Tree achieved a test accuracy of 84.21%, while Logistic Regression achieved 81.05%, making the Decision Tree slightly better overall. Analysis of unsafe occurrences showed that sections of the Palatlakaha River had the highest number of unsafe pH readings, followed by Church Lake, while Bugg Spring and parts of Turkey Lake recorded zero unsafe cases. This demonstrates how classification models combined with geospatial data can help identify water bodies with recurring pH safety issues.
